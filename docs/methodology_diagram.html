2

Pseudo-Absence Generation

Two competing strategies evaluated in parallel
🎲

Strategy A — Random Placement

Absence points drawn uniformly at random across the full study area extent.

  • No spatial constraints
  • 1:1 or configurable fire:absence ratio
  • Minimum distance buffer optional
⚠️

Known Issue

Points land in low-risk zones (cool, moist, non-vegetated) — environmentally unlike fire locations. Creates biased training data.

VS
🧠

Strategy B — Heuristic / GAN

Absence points generated to match the environmental profile of fire-prone areas.

  • GAN trained on fire-location covariate vectors
  • Generator produces realistic non-fire candidates
  • Heuristic rules as interpretable baseline
✅

Design Goal

Controls share fire-prone environmental characteristics — forcing the model to learn genuinely discriminative risk signals.

3

Pseudo-Absence Quality Validation

🔍

AUC Quality Test

A secondary binary classifier is trained solely to distinguish fire locations from the generated pseudo-absences. Its AUC reveals how "realistic" the controls are.

  • AUC ≈ 0.50 → controls indistinguishable from fire-prone areas ✓
  • AUC ≈ 0.90 → controls trivially different from fire locations ✗
→

🎲 Random Strategy

0.90
AUC Quality Score

Too easily separated — controls don't look like fire conditions. Training data is biased.

🧠 GAN / Heuristic

0.50
AUC Quality Score

Cannot be separated — controls match fire-prone environments. Training data is valid.

4

Predictive Model Training

🗃️

Dataset Assembly

Combine fire presences with each set of pseudo-absences to form labelled training datasets.

  • Feature extraction at each point
  • Train / validation / test split (e.g., 70/15/15)
  • Class-balance check
→
🤖

Classifier Training

Binary fire risk model trained independently on each absence strategy's dataset.

  • Algorithm: Random Forest / XGBoost / DNN
  • Hyperparameter tuning (cross-validation)
  • Trained twice: once per absence strategy
→
🗺️

Fire Risk Map

Model applied to full raster grid to produce a continuous fire probability surface.

  • Pixel-level probability [0–1]
  • Spatial visualization & hotspot detection
  • Produced for both strategies
5

Comparative Evaluation

📈
AUC-ROC

Discriminative power of each model on held-out fire test points

🎯
Precision / Recall

Trade-off between false alarms and missed detections at varying thresholds

🧩
Feature Importance

Which covariates drive predictions — and whether they are ecologically meaningful

🗺️
Spatial Validity

Visual inspection of prediction maps for geographic plausibility and artefacts

🏁

Key Research Finding

Models trained on GAN/heuristic pseudo-absences produce lower quality-test AUC scores (≈ 0.50) — confirming realistic controls — and yield fire risk maps with stronger ecological validity than random-absence baselines. Pseudo-absence placement strategy is the dominant source of model bias in fire occurrence modelling.
